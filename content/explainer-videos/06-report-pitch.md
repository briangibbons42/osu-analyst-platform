## Opening (0:00–0:20)

- The model is the engine, but the report and pitch are how you communicate your work.
- This video covers the report and pitch templates and how to use them to deliver your analysis.

## Report Template (0:20–1:20)

- The report follows a standard equity research structure, modeled on the club's existing output.
- Typical sections:
  - **Executive Summary** — Thesis, recommendation, target price.
  - **Company Overview** — What the company does, key products/services.
  - **Industry Analysis** — Market size, growth drivers, competitive dynamics.
  - **Financial Analysis** — Key metrics from your 3-statement model.
  - **Valuation** — DCF summary, sensitivity analysis, comparison to consensus.
  - **Risks** — What could go wrong? What are you monitoring?
  - **Appendix** — Detailed model tables, data sources.
- Each section has prompts — questions to answer rather than blanks to fill.

## Pitch Deck Template (1:20–2:10)

- The pitch distills your thesis into slides for a committee presentation.
- Typical slide flow:
  1. Title + recommendation (Buy/Hold/Sell, target price)
  2. Company snapshot
  3. Investment thesis and catalyst
  4. Key charts (revenue build, margin trend, segment mix)
  5. Valuation summary (DCF output, consensus comparison)
  6. Risks and monitoring
- Keep it concise — the pitch should communicate your view, not reproduce the report.

## Industry Deep Dive Checklist (2:10–2:50)

- A checklist for industry analysis:
  - Market size and growth
  - Key players and market share
  - Competitive advantages (moats)
  - Industry trends and disruptions
  - Regulatory environment
- Use this as a guide — not every company needs every section.

## Export (2:50–3:20)

- Export your report to Word/PDF and your pitch to PowerPoint.
- These match the club's current deliverable format (Word report + PPT pitch + Excel model).
- The optional "Example (Micron)" shows a completed pattern you can reference without copying.

## Close (3:20–3:40)

- A great model means nothing if you can't communicate it. The report and pitch are where you make your case.
