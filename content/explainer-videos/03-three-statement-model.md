## Opening (0:00–0:30)

- The 3-statement model is the backbone of equity research. It links the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement so changes flow through logically.
- Your model is built from SEC filings — real data, not guesses.

## Where the Numbers Come From (0:30–1:30)

- **10-K and 10-Q filings** on EDGAR contain audited financial statements.
- The platform loads historical data from these filings using XBRL (structured financial data).
- Walk through: Revenue → Cost of Revenue → Gross Profit → OpEx → Operating Income → Net Income.
- Each number has a source: "This came from the FY2024 10-K, filed on [date]."

## Segment and Geographic Detail (1:30–2:30)

- Companies report revenue and cost by business segment and geography in their 10-K footnotes.
- Example: Micron reports by DRAM, NAND, and Other segments; and by geography (US, China, etc.).
- Adding segment detail to your model gives you a more granular revenue build — which matters when your thesis is about a specific product or region.
- In Teach Mode, the platform explains where to find this in the filing.

## Management Guidance (2:30–3:15)

- Companies provide guidance on earnings calls and in filings — e.g., "We expect revenue of $X–$Y for next quarter."
- Guidance fields in the model let you record what management has said, so you can compare your assumptions to their targets.
- Source each piece of guidance: "Management FY2025 guidance, Q4 earnings call."

## Framing: Your Assumptions vs Consensus vs Management (3:15–4:15)

- For key drivers (revenue growth, gross margin, OpEx), the model shows three columns: **Your Assumption | Management | Consensus**.
- Your editable column drives the projected periods.
- When you differ from management or consensus, the platform highlights the divergence.
- This is critical: being explicit about where and why you differ is what makes research useful.

## Projected Periods (4:15–5:00)

- Historical periods are read-only (from filings). Projected periods are editable — these are your assumptions.
- Each projected number should tie back to your thesis and key assumptions.
- In Teach Mode, you do this one step at a time with prompts and hints. In Analyst Mode, you have full access.

## Linking to DCF (5:00–5:30)

- Revenue and Free Cash Flow from the 3-statement feed into the DCF.
- The platform makes this explicit: "This revenue assumption flows into your DCF projections."

## Close (5:30–5:50)

- The 3-statement model turns filings into a structured framework for testing your thesis.
- Next: use the DCF to translate your model into an implied valuation.

## Key Points (for in-app display)

- Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow are linked — changes flow through
- Historical data comes from SEC filings (XBRL); you map reported line items
- Segment and geographic detail adds granularity to revenue and cost projections
- Management guidance goes into dedicated fields for comparison
- Projected periods are driven by your assumptions — the framing columns show how they differ from consensus
