## Opening (0:00–0:25)

- A Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) values a company by projecting future free cash flows and discounting them back to today.
- It answers the question: "If my thesis is right, what is this company worth?"

## How it Links to the 3-Statement (0:25–1:00)

- Revenue projections and free cash flow come directly from the 3-statement model.
- The platform links them explicitly — no manual re-entry needed.
- When you change a revenue assumption in the 3-statement, the DCF updates.

## Key Inputs (1:00–2:30)

Walk through each input:

- **Revenue growth rates** (Year 1–5): Driven by your thesis. What does your revenue build look like?
- **EBITDA margins**: How efficient is the business at your projected scale?
- **CapEx (% of revenue)**: How much does the company need to invest to grow?
- **Net Working Capital change**: Cash tied up in operations.
- **Tax rate**: Usually the statutory rate unless you have a specific view.
- **WACC** (Weighted Average Cost of Capital): Your discount rate. Reflects the risk of the cash flows. Higher WACC = lower present value.
- **Terminal growth rate**: Growth rate in perpetuity after the explicit forecast period. Usually 2–3% (roughly GDP growth).

## Framing DCF Inputs (2:30–3:15)

- WACC and terminal growth are shown with framing columns: Your Assumption vs Consensus.
- Small changes in WACC or terminal growth have outsized impact on the implied price — the platform helps you see this.
- In Teach Mode, each input has an explanation of where it comes from and why it matters.

## Output (3:15–4:00)

- **Enterprise Value**: Sum of present value of projected FCFs + present value of terminal value.
- **Equity Value**: Enterprise Value minus net debt.
- **Implied Share Price**: Equity Value divided by shares outstanding.
- This is an output of your assumptions, not a prediction. It reflects what the company is worth *if your thesis is right*.

## Sensitivity (4:00–4:30)

- Change an input and watch the output move. This teaches you which assumptions drive the most value.
- Those high-impact assumptions are the ones to stress-test and defend in your pitch.

## Close (4:30–4:50)

- The DCF translates your thesis into a number. But the number is only as good as your assumptions.
- Next: compare your model outputs to consensus to see where you diverge from the Street.

## Key Points (for in-app display)

- DCF inputs (revenue, FCF) link directly from the 3-statement model
- WACC = weighted average cost of equity and debt — reflects risk
- Terminal value captures value beyond the explicit forecast period
- Growth assumptions should tie back to your thesis
- The implied share price is an output, not a prediction — it reflects your assumptions
