## Opening (0:00–0:20)

- Consolidated financials tell you the total picture, but segment and geographic data tell you *where* the money is coming from. Management guidance tells you *where management thinks it's going*.
- Both are essential for building a granular, defensible model.

## Finding Segment Data (0:20–1:15)

- **Where it lives:** 10-K, "Notes to Financial Statements" — look for "Segment Information" or "Business Segments."
- Also available in XBRL as structured segment tags.
- Example: Micron reports revenue by product type (DRAM, NAND) and by end market (compute, mobile, storage, etc.).
- Each segment often has its own revenue, cost, and operating income — giving you a bottom-up revenue build.

## Geographic Data (1:15–1:50)

- Companies also report revenue by geography — e.g., United States, China/Asia, Europe.
- This matters when your thesis involves a regional catalyst (e.g., data center buildout in the US, regulatory risk in China).
- Geographic revenue is usually in the same 10-K segment note or a separate geographic note.

## How Segments Tie to Your Model (1:50–2:30)

- In the model, instead of projecting one "Revenue" number, you can project by segment.
- Segment projections roll up to consolidated revenue.
- Teach Mode shows how this works: "DRAM revenue + NAND revenue + Other = Total Revenue."
- This gives you a more defensible projection because you're modeling the drivers, not just the total.

## Management Guidance (2:30–3:15)

- Companies give guidance on earnings calls, at investor days, and in filings.
- Common guidance: next-quarter revenue range, full-year margin targets, CapEx plans.
- In the model, guidance fields record what management has said and source it.
- Your projected numbers should be consciously above, below, or in line with guidance — and you should know why.

## Close (3:15–3:40)

- Segments and guidance add depth to your model. They turn "I think revenue will grow 15%" into "I think DRAM revenue will grow 20% driven by HBM ramp, while NAND is flat."
- That level of specificity is what makes equity research credible.

## Key Points (for in-app display)

- Segment data lives in 10-K/10-Q footnotes and XBRL tags
- Geographic revenue breakdown adds another dimension to your projections
- Management guidance comes from earnings calls, investor day, and filings
- Guidance fields in the model help you compare your assumptions to management's stated targets
